What is ELECTRONICS

Electronics engineering is the study of design, fabrication, and operation of circuits, electronic devices, and systems. It mostly deals with the development of automated machinery, to design electronic circuits, devices, VLSI devices and their systems. Students who study electronics engineering will develop an expert understanding of the circuits used in computers and other modern technologies, and for this reason electronics engineering is often taught alongside computer science. Moreover, this branch of engineering will also overlap with fundamental concepts and subject areas of mechanical and civil engineering.

Electronics engineering is further subdivided into many subfields, such as:

  • Signal Processing
  • Telecommunication Engineering
  • Electromagnetics
  • Control Engineering
  • Instrumentation Engineering
  • Computer Engineering
  • VLSI Design Engineering


Defination

electronics, branch of physics and electrical engineering that deals with the emission, behaviour, and effects of electrons and with electronic devices. Electronics encompasses an exceptionally broad range of technology. The term originally was applied to the study of electron behaviour and movement, particularly as observed in the first electron tubes. It came to be used in its broader sense with advances in knowledge about the fundamental nature of electrons and about the way in which the motion of these particles could be utilized. Today many scientific and technical disciplines deal with different aspects of electronics. Research in these fields has led to the development of such key devices as transistors, integrated circuits, lasers, and optical fibres. These in turn have made it possible to manufacture a wide array of electronic consumer, industrial, and military products. Career Opportunities for Electronics Engineers

Students who complete a degree in electronics engineering can go on to be eligible for several lucrative career opportunities. Electronics engineering imparts the knowledge and skills needed to fill in numerous roles and job positions.

Some popular career opportunities for electronics engineers in Pakistan are:

  • Broadcast engineer
  • Acoustic consultant
  • CAD technician
  • Control and instrumentation engineer
  • Design engineer
  • Electrical engineer
  • Electronics engineer
  • Sound engineer
  • IT consultant
  • Network engineer
  • Nuclear engineer
  • Special effects technician
  • Systems analyst
  • Aerospace engineer


Clearly, this field offers tons of opportunities for experience and prestigious job opportunities for pre-engineering intermediate students who have a passion for electronics, computers, and science. In order to successfully thrive in this field, intermediate students must first score well on their final exams and seek admission into a good program in Pakistan. Moreover, maintaining a good CGPA throughout the undergraduate program is necessary to develop a strong understanding of the field and in order to secure the best career opportunities.
